NELSON_MARKETING_PLAN.md

1. Purpose of This Plan
This document defines the positioning, audience, distribution strategy, and launch approach for Nelson.
The goal is not to create hype before the product exists. The goal is to ensure that when Nelson launches in the App Store, the message is clear, credible, and aligned with the product.  Marketing supports product launch. It does not run ahead of it.

2. Core Positioning
The Enemy

Streak culture.

Most habit apps punish imperfection. Miss one day and your streak resets to zero. This creates fragile systems where small mistakes cause people to quit entirely.

The Problem

The restart cycle.

People try to be perfect.
They slip once.
The streak breaks.
They restart Monday.

This cycle can repeat for years.

The Insight

Real behavior change does not operate like streaks. Real behavior change operates like momentum. Momentum survives imperfect days.

The Mechanism

Nelson measures and builds behavioral momentum, not streaks. Progress is measured across patterns of behavior rather than consecutive perfection.

Emotional Hook (Brand Narrative)

Never start over again.

This phrase represents the emotional insight behind Nelson and is used in founder storytelling and narrative content.

Placement rule:
This line should appear in storytelling, brand narrative, and educational content. It should not be used as a literal product promise in App Store descriptions or feature claims.

Functional Product Truth

One imperfect day doesn’t erase your progress.

This reflects how Nelson actually behaves as a system. Off days do not reset progress. Momentum persists across imperfect behavior.

Supporting Philosophy

Progress survives imperfect days.

3. The Product Truth

Marketing must remain aligned with what Nelson actually does.

Nelson is:

- a behavioral tracking system
- a momentum scoring engine
- a weekly coaching system that identifies patterns
- a tool for observing your habits over time

Nelson is not:

- a diet plan
- a calorie tracker
- a motivational streak game
- a productivity optimization tool

The product treats the user as a scientist observing their own patterns, not a subject being commanded what to do. Marketing should reflect that philosophy.

4. Target Audience
Primary User

Analytical adults (approximately 28–45) who:

- care about improving their health
- have tried habit systems before
- have experienced the restart cycle
- are open to understanding their behavior rather than chasing motivation

These users often:

- read about health or performance
- experiment with routines
- want data about their habits
- are frustrated with fragile habit systems

What Nelson is NOT targeting initially

- casual wellness app users
- extreme athletes
- people seeking prescriptive diet plans
- users wanting motivational cheerleading

Nelson attracts people who want honest feedback and behavioral awareness.

5. Founder Narrative

The founder story establishes credibility and authenticity. However, the story emphasizes the behavioral insight, not the body transformation.

Narrative Structure

The restart cycle
For years I lived in the Monday restart cycle.

The false belief
I believed consistency meant perfection.

The insight
The real problem was the system. One mistake reset everything.

The discovery
Real change operates through momentum.

The build
Nelson was built to measure and protect that momentum.

The 400-to-220 transformation supports the story as evidence, not the category Nelson belongs to.

6. Distribution Strategy

Marketing begins as message testing, not large-scale acquisition.

Tier 1 Channels
LinkedIn
Purpose: credibility and founder voice.

Content themes:

- the restart cycle
- perfection vs momentum
- the psychology of quitting
- lessons from long-term consistency

Cadence: approximately one post per week.

TikTok / Instagram Reels

Purpose: message testing and reach.

Content themes:
- short observations about streak failure
- examples of restart cycles
- personal experiences with consistency
- explaining the momentum concept

Cadence: one short video per week.

Tier 2 Channel
Reddit

Purpose: pain-language validation and early adopters.

Relevant communities include:

r/loseit
r/getdisciplined
r/decidingtobebetter
r/selfimprovement

Rule:
Lead with the insight about the restart cycle, not the product.

Participation should focus on sharing ideas and experiences about habit failure and consistency. The Nelson product should only be mentioned when it naturally arises in conversation or when directly asked about.

Tier 3 Amplifiers
Large Instagram Audience

A friend with a large Instagram following (300K+) can create a launch spike.

This should occur after the app is live, not during early waitlist stages.

Personal and Extended Networks

Helpful for early traction but not a primary acquisition strategy.

7. Pricing Strategy

Pricing should remain simple and signal premium value.

Founding Members (First 50 users)

Price: $60/year

Benefits:

- price locked while subscription remains active
- recognition as early supporters
- opportunity to influence product development

If a Founding Member cancels their subscription, the price lock is lost.

Standard Pricing

$99/year

or

$9/month

This pricing positions Nelson as a premium behavioral tool rather than a commodity habit app.

8. Launch Strategy

Marketing activity follows product readiness.

Phase 1 — Product Build

Focus:
- ship the native app
- refine onboarding
- ensure momentum scoring and coaching operate reliably

Marketing activity:

- light content testing
- refine narrative
- observe which ideas resonate

Phase 2 — Pre-Launch

When TestFlight or beta is stable:

- open waitlist
- onboard early testers
- collect testimonials
- refine Founding Member offer

Waitlist mechanics

- Traffic to the waitlist will come primarily from content posts on LinkedIn, TikTok, and Instagram.
- The incentive to join the waitlist is:
- early access to the app
- eligibility for Founding Member pricing
- Waitlist members will be notified via email when the app launches and Founding Member spots become available.

Phase 3 — Launch

When the product is App Store ready:

- announce Founding Members
- activate large social reach (300K IG post)
- direct users to download and subscribe

Goal:

First 50 Founding Members.  This validates pricing and product value.

9. Success Metrics

Early success is measured by:

- engagement with the product
- retention of early users
- conversion to Founding Members
- quality of user feedback
- Follower counts and vanity metrics are secondary.

10. Long-Term Vision

Nelson begins as a momentum system for health behavior.

Over time, the momentum model may expand to additional domains of personal behavior.

The initial launch remains focused on health consistency and behavioral awareness.

Solve one real problem well before expanding.

Status

Plan finalized and locked.

Further changes should only occur if:

the product architecture changes materially, or

early user feedback reveals major positioning problems.
